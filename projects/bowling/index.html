Multi-sport league platform Bowling first In development
Overview

Built for recurring league competition, not generic sports administration.

League-based sports still run on paper score sheets, disconnected spreadsheets, and manual reconciliation. Managers spend hours maintaining standings and league records, while players often have limited visibility into results, momentum, and season performance.

League Night replaces that patchwork with one connected system for score entry, automatic standings, roster participation, and league operations built around the way recurring competition actually works.

The first implementation focuses on bowling leagues, where weekly team matchups, live score entry, and standings movement are central to the experience. The platform architecture is intentionally designed to support additional league-based sports over time.

Platform direction

Bowling is the first implementation, not the long-term limit.

The platform begins with bowling because league night structure, recurring weekly play, and score-driven standings make it a strong first use case. The underlying architecture is being shaped for broader multi-sport expansion.
